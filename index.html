What is this tool?

Wordpress allows anyone to list all usernames of your website. It is as simple as running a custom script or using the WP REST API. This tool aims to check if your website is protected against such risk.

Why should I care?

First, it will help hackers to gain admin access during brute force attacks. Also consider that data privacy is a serious business (some members might use their email address as their ID). Lastly, you don't want to give extra and free information to your competitors, if any.

Who is concerned?

In 2017, almost 30% of websites are using Wordpress as a CMS, while Woocommerce claimed powering 30% of online stores back in 2015. Chances are you already used one of them as a developer or a business owner.

How can I fix this?

A simple, quick and free action you can take is to install a plugin called Stop User Enumeration. Keep in mind that you can get way more information with appropriate tools, this website has been designed to sensitize people to common security exploits.
